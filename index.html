The problem

Feedback is everywhere and nowhere.

Your users are telling you what they want. But you can't hear them over the noise.

Before Loopline

  • No way to know which request represents 1 user vs. 100

  • Users assume you ignored them — they churn

With Loopline

  • Public roadmap users can follow and trust
  • Changelog auto-closes linked requests with "you asked, we built it"
  • Users feel heard — they stay and refer others

Changelog

"You asked, we built it."

When you publish a changelog entry and link it to feedback posts, Loopline automatically closes those posts as Shipped and notifies voters. Users who asked for the feature hear about it first — turning your changelog into a loyalty engine, not just a release log.

AI-assisted

Boards that don't turn into landfills.

As a user types a new request, Loopline runs a semantic similarity search (powered by pgvector embeddings — free, on your own database) and surfaces "Looks like this might already exist." They upvote the existing post instead of creating a duplicate. Your board stays clean without manual triaging.

Inline admin

Change status without leaving the board.

When you're logged in as an admin, status controls appear right on the post. No separate admin panel to open, no context-switching, no "go to admin view first." Just click the status badge, pick the new one, and you're done. It's the workflow nobody else bothered to build.
